My 7-Day NSS Special Camp Experience

​In early 2024, I spent seven days at an NSS Special Camp in Navgaon with Dev Bhoomi Uttarakhand University, and looking back, it was a pretty eye-opening experience. On the final day, I actually stood up in front of everyone to share my thoughts, and it really made me think about how much you can change and learn in just one short week. ​One of the biggest highlights was going on village visits. We went door-to-door just talking with the local residents, listening to the everyday challenges they face, and trying to help out by sharing useful info about basic healthcare and technology. We also organized a 'Beti Bachao Beti Padhao' rally through the village to advocate for girl child education and empowerment. It was incredibly grounding to see how much people appreciate you just showing up and listening. ​Living at the camp also forced us to learn real teamwork and ground logistics. At one point, my team and I were responsible for managing the camp kitchen and cooking daily meals for over 60 people! It was chaotic but incredibly fun, and it taught me a lot about patience and coordination under pressure. We also did a lot of heavy lifting during our Swachhata cleanliness drives, working together to clean up public spaces and improve rural hygiene. ​On a personal level, the camp was a huge stepping stone for me. Getting away from my usual routine meant my daily screen time completely dropped, which was a massive relief. Instead of staring at a phone, I was building real connections, learning to listen better, and working on my communication skills. Standing on that stage on the seventh day and speaking with confidence showed me how much my public speaking has improved. ​This camp really brought the core NSS motto to life: "Not Me, But You." It taught me that real leadership isn't about standing above people; it’s about working right alongside them to make a difference.
